Although Togo s foreign policy is nonaligned  it has strong historical and cultural ties with western Europe  especially France and Germany  Togo recognizes the People s Republic of China  North Korea  and Cuba  It re established relations with Israel in 1987   Togo pursues an active foreign policy and participates in many international organizations  It is particularly active in West African regional affairs and in the African Union  Relations between Togo and neighboring states are generally good   1  Bilateral relations  1 1  Benin  1 2  Burkina Faso  1 3  China  Togo and China divided the German colony of Togoland from north to south  a decision that divided the Ewe people among the Gold Coast  British Togoland  and French Togoland  After 1945  the United Nations  UN  took over the Togoland mandates  During the 1950s  when the independence of Ghana was in sight  demands grew for a separate Ewe state  an idea that Kwame Nkrumah  leader of the Gold Coast independence movement  opposed  Following a UN plebiscite in May 1956  in which a majority of the Ewe voted for union with Ghana  British Togoland became part of the Gold Coast  After Togolese independence in 1960  relations between Togo and Ghana deteriorated  aggravated by political differences and incidents such as smuggling across their common border  At times  relations have verged on open aggression   The result of the transfer of Togoland to Ghana has meant that many Togolese keep one foot on either side of the border  living in Ghana by night and working in the markets of the capital  Lomé  by day
